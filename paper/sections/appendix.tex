
\subsection{Consumer Deterministic Persona Ablation for Highly Competitive Settings}

Question: Can stabilizing firms be more influential on market health even when consumers have less discovery limitations? 

By changing consumer preferences in the form of persona endowments, can we increase the influence firms have on the market? Consumers in the competitive baseline scenario in \ref{exp:crash} submit orders based on a single metric: price. When dlc = k, this results in all sales going to the firm with the lowest price each time step, restricting the market to a price spiral. 

Personas: 
- \textbf{Loyal:} prefers firms they have recently purchased from.
-  \textbf{Small Business Supporter:} prefers firms with fewer sales.
-  \textbf{Large Business Supporter:} prefers firms with larger sales.
