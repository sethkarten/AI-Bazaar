\begin{abstract}
  The deployment of Large Language Models (LLMs) as autonomous economic agents introduces systemic risks that extend beyond individual capability failures. As agents transition to high-frequency marketplaces (e.g., Amazon, Moltbook), their collective behavior can amplify volatility and mask deception at scale. We introduce \textbf{Agent Bazaar}, a multi-agent simulation framework for evaluating \textit{Economic Alignment}---defined as the capacity of agentic systems to preserve market stability and integrity. We identify two emergent failure modes: (1) \textit{Algorithmic Instability} ("The Crash"), where myopic firms amplify price volatility, and (2) \textit{Sybil Deception} ("The Lemon Market"), where coordinated networks of deceptive agents flood the market with fraudulent listings. We demonstrate that standard open-weights models fail to self-regulate, leading to market collapse. To address this, we propose a dual-alignment strategy: finetuning \textit{Sustainable Firms} to act as volatility dampeners, and \textit{Skeptical Guardians} to detect collusion patterns in seller networks. Our results show that aligned agents do not just maximize profit; they restore equilibrium and protect human welfare in non-stationary environments.
\end{abstract}
